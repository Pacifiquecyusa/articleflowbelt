Finally! How To Get Comfortable Sleep During Long Flights Without Even Paying Extra $$$ for Business Class

Perfect for avoiding neck pain, head bobbing, and uncomfortable sleep positions while traveling.

By Emma Cowan

PUBLISHED: DEC 2, 2022

SAVE ARTICLE

All the links provided on this page are based on products we've personally tested and love.

When it comes to traveling, sleeping anywhere but in a bed is my personal nightmare. I'm Emma, and I've been a travel writer for a decade, exploring over thirty countries and a hundred destinations. While I love the adventure, the journey often turns into a contorted saga of neck pain and exhaustion.

To maximize my time at destinations and save money, I often choose inconvenient flight times, leading to sleepless, stressful trips. I've tried numerous travel pillows, from cushioned to memory foam, but none have worked.

Live footage of me after trying them all, but none could give me a good rest.

The issue with all other pillows I've tried is that they're not *supportive*. My head ends up bobbing around like a dashboard dog. I'll be dozing off, my head will jerk to the side and then I'm back awake.

The genius of this pillow lies in its design: a neck brace wrapped in cozy fabric. It has a foam-cushioned support that props your head up in an ergonomic position for better sleep. Here I am demonstrating on a recent flight, like a true pro:

This support has actually been *scientifically proven to be better for sleep* – as found by Dundee University's Department of Orthopaedic and Trauma Surgery.

The support attaches to a long fabric strip, like a scarf, which you wrap around your neck and fasten with a soft touch closure.

The fleecy fabric is super soft and also hypoallergenic which is great for me as I have sensitive skin. And it's also machine washable which is great for my personal hygiene levels while travelling.

Natural sleeping position without neck cramps

You can choose to wear it in multiple different positions depending on your sleeping position (left side, right side, under your chin, the choice is yours, folks).

Whatever your sleeping position, it gets rid of neck cramps and the awful feeling of your head suddenly falling towards your shoulder.

It's also really easy to transport. A lot of cushioned travel pillows are really bulky, while inflatable ones are just a massive faff in my opinion. This weighs just 128 grams and slips nicely into my bag.

Gotta love a delicious lil' nap.

In conclusion, if you struggle to sleep when you're travelling and find early morning or overnight flights a hellish nightmare of fitful dozing, try this travel pillow. I've now been using it for two years and it's still the best option I've ever found.
